Programme Overview

Conducted under the CU Space Technology Initiative, the internship bridged the gap between academic learning and real-world engineering. The 25-day intensive learning experience provided students with direct exposure to satellite engineering, space technologies, and interactions with leading scientists from India's premier space organizations.

Participants received comprehensive hands-on training across multiple domains including Satellite Systems Engineering, Spacecraft Architecture, Embedded Systems, Artificial Intelligence for Space, Remote Sensing, Ground Segment Technologies, and UAV Systems.

Collaborating Organizations

The programme was marked by strong collaborations with the Department of Space (DoS) and leading national organizations. More than 15 distinguished scientists, engineers, and academicians directly mentored the students.

Flagship Activity: Satellite Engineering Boot Camp

A landmark achievement of the programme was the academic collaboration with the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST). For the first time, IIST partnered with Chandigarh University to conduct a rigorous Satellite Engineering Boot Camp under the guidance of Prof. Priyadarshnam (Head, SSPACE, IIST).

Joint Certification: Students who successfully completed the Boot Camp were awarded prestigious joint certificates bearing the logos of both Chandigarh University and IIST.

Ceremonies & Leadership

Inaugural Ceremony

Held on 15 June 2026, the programme was inaugurated by Shri Sudhakar, Distinguished Scientist & Deputy Director at URSC, ISRO, serving as the Chief Guest. The event underscored the University's commitment to advancing space education.

Valedictory Function

Concluded on 10 July 2026 with Dr. M. Nageswara Rao, Distinguished Scientist and former Satish Dhawan Professor (ISRO), as the Chief Guest. Student projects were showcased and joint IIST certificates were distributed.

Programme Outcomes

  • Provided practical exposure to Satellite Systems Engineering and spacecraft technologies.
  • Developed core competencies in systems engineering, payload integration, and mission planning.
  • Fostered strong industry-academia-government collaboration for future initiatives like CU-Sat and StratoSat.
  • Positioned the Kalpana Chawla Centre as an emerging national hub for Space Science education.
